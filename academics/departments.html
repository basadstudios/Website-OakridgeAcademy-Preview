English & Language Arts

Department Overview

Our English Department fosters critical reading, effective writing, and thoughtful analysis of literature from diverse authors and traditions. Students develop communication skills essential for academic and professional success.

Course Offerings

World Literature

Grades 9-10

Exploration of literary traditions from around the world, examining cultural contexts and universal themes.

American Literature

Grade 11

Study of American literary movements, major authors, and cultural influences from colonial times to the present.

British Literature

Grade 12

Survey of British literature from Anglo-Saxon period through modernism, focusing on historical and cultural contexts.

Creative Writing

Elective

Workshop-based course developing skills in fiction, poetry, drama, and creative non-fiction.

AP English Language

Grade 11

College-level course focusing on rhetorical analysis, argument, and synthesis of information from multiple sources.

AP English Literature

Grade 12

Advanced study of literary analysis through close reading of texts from various genres and periods.

Dr. Emily Chen

Department Chair

Ph.D. in English Literature from Columbia University with 15 years of teaching experience. Published author specializing in contemporary American literature.

Mathematics

Department Overview

Our Mathematics Department builds strong foundations in mathematical reasoning, problem-solving, and analytical thinking. From algebra to calculus, students develop skills applicable across disciplines and in real-world contexts.

Course Offerings

Algebra I

Grade 9

Fundamental algebraic concepts including equations, functions, and problem-solving strategies.

Geometry

Grade 9-10

Study of shapes, sizes, properties of space, and formal mathematical proofs.

Algebra II

Grade 10-11

Advanced algebraic concepts, functions, and introduction to trigonometry.

Pre-Calculus

Grade 11-12

Preparation for calculus covering functions, analytic geometry, and trigonometry.

AP Calculus AB

Grade 11-12

College-level calculus covering limits, derivatives, integrals, and their applications.

AP Statistics

Grade 11-12

Introduction to major concepts of collecting, analyzing, and drawing conclusions from data.

Dr. Robert Kim

Department Chair

Ph.D. in Mathematics from MIT with specialization in applied mathematics. Former research mathematician with 12 years of teaching experience.

Science

Department Overview

Our Science Department emphasizes inquiry-based learning, laboratory investigation, and scientific literacy. Students explore the natural world through biology, chemistry, physics, and specialized electives.

Course Offerings

Biology

Grade 9

Study of living organisms, their structure, function, growth, and interactions with the environment.

Chemistry

Grade 10

Exploration of matter, its properties, composition, and the changes it undergoes during reactions.

Physics

Grade 11

Investigation of energy, forces, motion, and the fundamental laws governing the physical world.

Environmental Science

Elective

Interdisciplinary study of environmental systems, human impacts, and sustainability.

AP Biology

Grade 11-12

College-level biology covering molecular and cellular biology, genetics, evolution, and ecology.

AP Chemistry

Grade 11-12

Advanced study of chemical principles, reactions, and analysis with extensive laboratory work.

Dr. Marcus Colombo

Department Chair

Ph.D. in Chemistry from Stanford University with research background in biochemistry. Leads our award-winning science program with focus on research skills.

Social Studies

Department Overview

Our Social Studies Department explores human societies, cultures, and institutions through history, geography, economics, and government. Students develop critical thinking about past and present social issues.

Course Offerings

World History

Grade 9

Survey of major civilizations, cultural developments, and global interactions throughout history.

U.S. History

Grade 11

Comprehensive study of American history from pre-colonial times to the present.

Government & Economics

Grade 12

Examination of political systems, civic participation, and fundamental economic principles.

Global Studies

Elective

Analysis of contemporary global issues, international relations, and cultural perspectives.

AP U.S. History

Grade 11

College-level American history with emphasis on critical analysis of primary sources.

AP Human Geography

Elective

Study of patterns and processes that have shaped human understanding and use of Earth's surface.

Department Chair

French I-IV

Mandarin I-IV

Latin I-IV
