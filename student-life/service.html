Community Service

Making a difference through meaningful service and social responsibility.

Service Learning Philosophy

At Oakridge Academy, we believe that community service is an essential part of education. Through our service learning program, students develop empathy, leadership, and a sense of social responsibility while making meaningful contributions to their communities.

Our approach integrates classroom learning with community engagement, allowing students to apply academic concepts to real-world challenges while developing a lifelong commitment to service.

Program Structure

1

Grade-Level Requirements

Each grade level has specific service learning requirements that build in complexity and commitment:

  • Freshmen: 15 hours with guided group projects
  • Sophomores: 20 hours with choice of school-sponsored opportunities
  • Juniors: 25 hours with focus on sustained engagement
  • Seniors: 30 hours including leadership and independent projects
2

Reflection & Integration

Service learning goes beyond volunteering by connecting community engagement with academic reflection:

  • Weekly advisory discussions
  • Service journals and written reflections
  • Integration with curriculum in all subject areas
  • Annual student-led service symposium
3

Leadership Opportunities

Students can develop leadership skills through our service program:

  • Student Service Council membership
  • Service project leadership positions
  • Peer mentorship for underclassmen
  • Community partnership liaisons

Local Partnerships

Regular volunteer opportunities with local organizations addressing community needs.

City Food Bank

Students sort, package, and distribute food to families facing food insecurity.

Impact: Provides meals to over 500 families each month

Frequency: Weekly

Riverside Elementary Tutoring

Students tutor elementary school children in reading, math, and science.

Impact: Supports 100+ elementary students each year

Frequency: Twice Weekly

Senior Center Connections

Students visit with seniors, lead activities, and provide companionship.

Impact: Builds relationships with 50+ seniors

Frequency: Bi-weekly

Habitat for Humanity

Students assist with building and renovating homes for families in need.

Impact: Contributed to 5 homes in the past year

Frequency: Monthly

Environmental Initiatives

Projects focused on environmental conservation and sustainability.

City Park Cleanup

Students remove litter and invasive species from local parks.

Impact: Maintains 4 city parks throughout the year

Frequency: Monthly

Community Garden

Students maintain a garden that provides fresh produce to the food bank.

Impact: Produces 2,000+ pounds of fresh produce annually

Frequency: Weekly

River Watershed Project

Students monitor water quality and participate in restoration projects.

Impact: Helps protect 30 miles of river ecosystem

Frequency: Bi-weekly

Sustainable Campus Initiative

Students lead sustainability projects on campus, including recycling and energy conservation.

Impact: Reduced campus waste by 25% in the past year

Frequency: Ongoing

Global Outreach

Initiatives that connect our students to global issues and communities.

International Service Trip

Annual trip to partner with communities on infrastructure, education, or conservation projects.

Impact: 30 students participate each year

Frequency: Annual

Global Water Project

Fundraising and awareness campaign to support clean water access globally.

Impact: Funded 10 water projects in developing countries

Frequency: Year-round

Refugee Support Initiative

Students support refugee families resettling in our community.

Impact: Supported 15 families in their transition

Frequency: Weekly

Sister School Partnership

Exchange program and support for a partner school in Guatemala.

Impact: Ongoing relationship for over 10 years

Frequency: Year-round
