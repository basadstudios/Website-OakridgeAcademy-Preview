7:T79f,Last weekend, Oakridge Academy was transformed into a global village for our Annual International Cultural Festival. This beloved tradition celebrates the diverse backgrounds and heritage of our student community, which represents over 30 different countries and cultures.

The festival featured student-led cultural exhibits, traditional performances, an international food court, and interactive workshops. Families and community members joined our students and staff in this vibrant celebration of global citizenship and cultural appreciation.

"This event is so much more than a festival – it's an educational experience," explains Mrs. Fatima Chaudhry, World Languages Department Chair and event coordinator. "Students spend weeks researching their heritage or chosen cultures, preparing authentic presentations, and learning to share their traditions with pride."

Highlights included traditional dance performances from India, Mexico, and Ghana; a fashion show featuring traditional attire from around the world; and a culinary journey through global cuisines prepared by student-parent teams. Workshops offered attendees the chance to learn Chinese calligraphy, African drumming, and traditional craft techniques from various cultures.

The festival also featured a keynote address from Dr. Maya Sen, a renowned cultural anthropologist and Oakridge parent, who spoke about the importance of cultural understanding in today's interconnected world.

"What makes Oakridge special is how we don't just acknowledge diversity – we celebrate it as a strength," said Dr. James Anderson, Principal. "This festival exemplifies our commitment to raising globally-minded citizens who appreciate different perspectives and can navigate our multicultural world with respect and understanding."
