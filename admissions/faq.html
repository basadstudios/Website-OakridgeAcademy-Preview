General Questions

What makes Oakridge Academy unique?

Oakridge Academy offers a rigorous academic program that emphasizes critical thinking, creativity, and character development. Our small class sizes, experienced faculty, and supportive community create an environment where students thrive academically, socially, and emotionally. We combine traditional educational values with innovative approaches to prepare students for success in college and beyond.

What grade levels do you offer?

We offer a comprehensive educational program for students in Kindergarten through Grade 12, divided into three divisions: Lower School (K-5), Middle School (6-8), and Upper School (9-12).

What is your average class size?

Our average class size is 15 students, with a student-to-teacher ratio of 8:1. This allows our teachers to provide personalized attention and support to each student.

Do you offer transportation services?

Yes, we offer bus transportation for students living within a 15-mile radius of the school. Transportation fees are not included in tuition and are billed separately.

Are students required to wear uniforms?

Yes, all students are required to wear school uniforms. The uniform policy varies by division and is designed to promote a sense of community and reduce distractions in the learning environment.

Admissions Process

When should I apply for admission?

We accept applications beginning in September for the following academic year. Our priority application deadline is January 15, but we continue to accept applications on a rolling basis as long as space is available.

What are the application requirements?

The application process includes: 1) Completed online application, 2) Application fee, 3) Student records/transcripts, 4) Teacher recommendations, 5) Student assessment/testing, and 6) Family interview. Requirements vary by grade level, so please visit our How to Apply page for detailed information.

Do you offer admission throughout the year?

While most students enter at the beginning of the academic year, we do consider mid-year applications when space is available.

Is there an entrance exam?

Yes, applicants for grades K-12 complete age-appropriate assessments as part of the admissions process. Lower School applicants take a developmental assessment, while Middle and Upper School applicants take a standardized admission test.

When will I receive an admissions decision?

For applications received by the January 15 priority deadline, admissions decisions are sent in early March. For applications received after the priority deadline, decisions are made on a rolling basis as space allows.

Tuition & Financial Aid

What is the tuition for the current academic year?

Tuition varies by grade level. For the 2024-2025 academic year, tuition ranges from $24,850 for Lower School to $29,850 for Upper School. Please visit our Tuition & Aid page for detailed information.

Are there additional costs beyond tuition?

Yes, there are some additional costs including mandatory fees, books, supplies, uniforms, and optional expenses like after-school programs, trips, and lunch service.

Do you offer financial aid?

Yes, we are committed to making our educational programs accessible to qualified students from diverse socioeconomic backgrounds. We offer need-based financial aid and flexible payment options. Approximately 25% of our students receive some form of financial assistance.

How do I apply for financial aid?

Families apply for financial aid through School and Student Services (SSS), a third-party service that helps schools determine financial need. The application requires tax returns and other financial information. For priority consideration, applications should be submitted by February 1.

Are there payment plans available?

Yes, we offer several payment options including annual, semi-annual, and monthly payment plans to help families manage tuition costs.

Academic Program

What curriculum do you follow?

Our college-preparatory curriculum is designed to challenge and engage students while developing critical thinking, creativity, and communication skills. We offer a rigorous core program in mathematics, science, English, social studies, and world languages, complemented by robust offerings in the arts, athletics, and technology.

Do you offer Advanced Placement (AP) courses?

Yes, our Upper School offers over 20 AP courses across all academic disciplines. Many of our students take multiple AP courses during their high school years.

What foreign languages do you offer?

We offer Spanish, French, Mandarin Chinese, and Latin. Lower School students have introductory language exposure, while Middle and Upper School students take language courses as part of their regular curriculum.

How do you support students with different learning needs?

Our Learning Support program provides resources for students with mild to moderate learning differences. The program includes individualized support, accommodations, and regular progress monitoring to ensure all students can access our curriculum and achieve success.

What is your approach to technology in the classroom?

We integrate technology thoughtfully across the curriculum to enhance learning, develop digital literacy, and prepare students for an increasingly digital world. Middle and Upper School students participate in our 1:1 device program, while Lower School students have access to shared devices and technology resources.

Student Life

What extracurricular activities do you offer?

We offer a wide range of extracurricular activities including clubs, sports teams, arts programs, community service opportunities, and academic competitions. With over 40 clubs and activities, students can explore their interests, develop leadership skills, and connect with peers who share their passions.

What sports are offered?

Our athletic program includes basketball, soccer, volleyball, cross country, swimming, tennis, track and field, baseball, softball, lacrosse, and golf. Most sports are available at both varsity and junior varsity levels.

Are arts programs available?

Yes, we have comprehensive visual and performing arts programs including studio art, digital media, choir, band, orchestra, and theater. Students can participate in these programs both during the school day and through extracurricular activities.

Do you have after-school programs?

Yes, we offer Extended Day programs for Lower and Middle School students until 6:00 PM. These programs include homework help, enrichment activities, and supervised play.

How do you promote character development and leadership?

Character development and leadership are integrated throughout our program through our advisory system, community service requirements, leadership opportunities, and social-emotional learning curriculum. We emphasize integrity, responsibility, empathy, and ethical decision-making.

College Preparation & Outcomes

What is your college matriculation rate?

100% of our graduates are accepted to four-year colleges and universities. Over the past five years, our students have been accepted to all eight Ivy League schools and many other selective colleges and universities across the country.

How does your college counseling program work?

Our comprehensive college counseling program begins in 9th grade and becomes increasingly individualized as students progress through high school. Our dedicated college counselors work closely with students and families to identify best-fit colleges, navigate the application process, and support students in their transition to higher education.

What is your AP pass rate?

Our AP pass rate (scores of 3 or higher) is consistently above 90%, with more than 70% of our students earning scores of 4 or 5.

What are your average SAT/ACT scores?

The middle 50% of our students score between 1350-1480 on the SAT and 30-34 on the ACT.

Do students receive assistance with standardized test preparation?

Yes, we offer SAT/ACT preparation workshops and resources to help students perform their best on these important tests. Our regular curriculum also naturally prepares students for these assessments through its emphasis on critical thinking, reading comprehension, writing skills, and mathematical reasoning.
